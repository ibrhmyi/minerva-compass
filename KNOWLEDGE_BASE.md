## About Minerva University

Minerva University is an independent, non-profit educational institution headquartered in San Francisco, California. It is accredited by the WASC Senior College and University Commission (WSCUC) and authorized by the Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP) to host international students on an F-1 visa for the undergraduate residential program.

Minerva is ranked the #1 Most Innovative University in the World for 2022, 2023, and 2024 by the World University Rankings for Innovation (WURI).

**Mission:** To develop problem-solvers, entrepreneurs, and wise leaders from around the globe; and weave them together as one community to address the greatest challenges facing humanity.

**Key Facts:**
- 85% of students come from outside the U.S.
- 100+ countries represented in the student body
- 11:1 student-to-faculty ratio
- All classes are small seminars with fewer than 23 students
- Classes are live, synchronous, and conducted on Minerva's interactive learning platform
- Minerva is first and foremost a residential program — students live together in global rotation cities

**Address:** 14 Mint Plaza, Suite 300, San Francisco, CA 94103
**General Email:** info@minerva.edu

## Minerva's Teaching Approach (Pedagogy)

Minerva uses a four-part learning cycle:

1. **Prepare** — Students engage with readings, videos, simulations, or problem sets before class to build foundational knowledge (inverted classroom model)
2. **Engage** — Live, virtual seminars with small groups featuring fast-paced discussions, debates, simulations, and problem-solving exercises guided by professors trained in active learning. Every student is heard, every student contributes, and no one fades into the background.
3. **Apply** — Students apply what they learn through hands-on projects embedded into courses and through civic projects. Projects might include research, design challenges, consulting for startups, or community-based work with local partners. Projects are not an add-on — they're how students internalize skills.
4. **Impact** — Global collaboration creating tangible results in rotation cities.

The curriculum centers on "Habits of Mind and Foundational Concepts" — cognitive tools like systems thinking and evidence-based reasoning, plus essential ideas from multiple disciplines.

## Global Immersion / Rotation Cities

Students live and learn across four continents over their undergraduate years. The global rotation is central to Minerva's mission: preparing students to solve the world's most complex challenges.

**Four-Year Rotation Structure:**
- **Year 1:** San Francisco — A vibrant hub of innovation and social change
- **Year 2:** Tokyo — Where tradition meets cutting-edge technology (beginning Fall 2025)
- **Years 3 & 4:** Rotating cities — Cities change annually. Past rotation cities include Berlin, Buenos Aires, Hyderabad, Taipei, Seoul, and London, each with unique histories and systems that expand perspective and sharpen skills.

**Residence Halls:** Located in vibrant urban neighborhoods with access to markets, shops, cafes, and restaurants. San Francisco halls are in mid-Market and Nob Hill neighborhoods.

**Academic Integration:**

- **Civic Projects:** Collaborate with renowned companies, nonprofits, government agencies, and startups tackling urgent local issues
- **Global Labs:** Project-based learning that challenges students to design, test, and iterate solutions with real-world stakeholders

**What Graduates Gain:**
- Lived in at least four global cities
- Navigated diverse civic and economic systems
- Applied knowledge across cultures through hands-on projects
- Created a global network

## Application Structure — Three Parts

### Part 1: Biographical & Academic Information (30-40 minutes)
- Basic personal and academic data
- Parental/guardian contact required for applicants under 18
- Can be completed via Minerva's website OR Common App (but Common App only replaces Part 1 and offers no advantage; Parts 2 and 3 must be completed on Minerva's site regardless)

### Part 2: Minerva Challenges (~2 hours total)
Online interactive challenges replacing standardized tests. Measures how you THINK, not what you KNOW.

**Timed Challenges (~8-20 minutes each):**
- Mathematics
- Logical Reasoning
- Reading Comprehension
- Divergent Thinking

**Communication Tasks (~20 minutes each):**
- Written communication exploring perspectives
- Verbal communication tasks

**Requirements:**
- Desktop or laptop computer with stable internet connection
- Webcam and microphone
- Calm, focused environment with 1.5-2 hours of quiet time
- Must be completed in English (no translation)

**Important Rules:**
- You CANNOT study for these — there is nothing to memorize
- NO outside resources allowed (no AI, websites, or external sources)
- Some include brief practice sessions
- Paper and pencil allowed for note-taking
- Can be completed in one sitting or across multiple sessions before deadline
- Don't procrastinate — delaying gives no advantage
- Contact admissions@minerva.edu with screenshots for technical issues
- Special accommodations: email disabilityservices@minerva.edu BEFORE starting timed portions

### Part 3: Accomplishments & Academic Records

**Accomplishments (up to 6):**
- Minimum 3 required; successful applicants typically submit 4+
- Each needs: summary, detailed description, supporting evidence, validator contact
- Evidence: photos, videos, social media, newspaper articles, certificates, diplomas, website links
- Validator: a non-family contact who can verify claims
- Acceptable accomplishments include: personal projects or creative work, academic competitions or artistic pursuits, leadership roles, community involvement, work experience

**Tips for Strong Accomplishments:**
1. ELABORATE with quantifiable impact (specific numbers: competitors faced, people led, money raised, audience reached)
2. Include PERSONAL achievements (initiative, community impact, creations, obstacles overcome)
3. Provide EVIDENCE (documentation, media coverage, certificates)
4. Submit at LEAST 4 accomplishments for breadth

**Weak example:** "Helped with school charity show" (vague, no numbers, no role clarity)
**Strong example:** "As VP of student council at a 950-student school, led 10-person team organizing charity show, raised 33,000 rupees, covered by The Bangalore Times" (specific role, numbers, outcome, media validation)

**Academic Records:**
- Three years of recent academic records (may span multiple institutions)
- Unofficial transcript from applicant initially
- Official verification required from school counselor or registrar
- Shows coursework rigor and progression
- AP/college courses don't allow placing out of Minerva courses (Cornerstone courses don't cover AP content)
- Dual-enrollment and international program transcripts should be included

## Financial Aid

### Core Principles
Minerva's financial aid operates on four pillars:
1. **Learning-Focused Investment** — investing only in what enhances learning
2. **Fair Share Contribution** — students contribute based on ability
3. **Global Philanthropic Support** — relies on private donors rather than U.S. federal aid
4. **Equitable Access** — offers aid regardless of residency or citizenship

### Key Facts
- ALL students regardless of citizenship are eligible for financial aid
- Financial aid is NEED-AWARE (ability to pay is considered in final admission recommendations)
- Over 70% of enrolled students receive some form of financial aid
- $0 in U.S. federal aid utilized — entirely privately funded
- Minerva does NOT offer merit-based scholarships or full-ride packages
- No full rides offered; all students pay a base Expected Family Contribution (EFC) each semester
- Students who have already earned an undergraduate degree may only qualify for low-interest student loans

### Aid Components
- **Need-Based Scholarships:** Grant funding requiring no repayment
- **Work-Study Program:** Students can earn up to $5,000 annually while gaining work experience
- **Low-Interest Student Loans:** Affordable financing through Climb Credit

### Required Documents for Financial Aid Application
1. **CSS Profile** (required for ALL students including international) — Minerva School Code: 6033
2. **Minerva Financial Aid Questionnaire** (submitted through Financial Aid Center after CSS Profile)
3. **Income Statements:** 2023 and 2024 tax returns, W-2s, or foreign equivalents
4. **Parents' Bank Statements:** 12-month itemized statements for ALL accounts (checking, savings, investment) showing account holder name, date range, and total available
5. **3 months of income statements** from employer or self-employment records
- FAFSA is NOT required
- International students are NOT eligible for CSS profile fee waivers
- US citizens may qualify for CSS waivers if: family AGI ≤$100,000, qualify for SAT fee waivers, or are orphans/wards under 24
- Parental financial info required for all students (exceptions only for: 26+ years old, married, legal dependents, orphans/wards)
- Non-custodial parent info IS required even if not contributing financially

### Financial Aid Deadlines
- Tied to admissions cycle — due ONE WEEK after admissions deadline
- Binding Enrollment: within 5 days of submitting application
- NO deadline extensions
- Late applications will NOT be accepted

### Annual Review & Appeals
- Aid receives yearly evaluation based on updated financial information
- Families may appeal if circumstances change significantly (job loss, medical expenses, etc.) with documentation

### External Scholarships
Minerva does not participate in U.S. Government Title IV financial aid. Students are encouraged to research external scholarships from organizations like the Masason Foundation, Last Mile Fund, Free the Children Trust, Amazon, and Adobe Circle Scholarship.

## 4-Year Curriculum

**Average class size:** 22 students

### Year 1 — Foundation (San Francisco)
Cornerstone courses develop four core competencies that underpin everything students learn for the rest of their Minerva career:

- **Formal Analyses (CS51):** Critical thinking through in-depth training in logic, statistics, and algorithmic thinking, using formal, computational, and quantitative approaches to formulate, analyze, and tackle problems.
- **Multimodal Communications (AH51):** Effective high-level communication — public speaking, visual communication, design, debate, and artistic expression. Students learn to deeply analyze communications of various forms.
- **Empirical Analyses (NS51):** Creative thinking through social and natural science methods — effectively framing problems, identifying solutions, developing and testing hypotheses, designing research studies, and reaching plausible conclusions.
- **Complex Systems (SS51):** Effective interaction — exploring how humans function as members of various systems, using concepts such as multiple causality and multi-factor integrations. Includes group collaboration, negotiation, leadership, and formal debate.

### Year 2 — Direction (First Rotation City)
Students declare their major in the second semester of first year and take Core courses in their chosen field. Five majors available:
- Arts & Humanities — Apply creative human thought to understanding events, ideas, and policies
- Business — Develop understanding of corporate dynamics, strategy, and global business success
- Computational Sciences — Harness data analysis and computational methods including AI tools
- Natural Sciences — Gain knowledge for leadership in scientific and technological organizations
- Social Sciences — Use research to inform public policies and solve societal challenges

### Year 3 — Focus (Second/Third Rotation City)
Students choose a concentration within their major (or design a custom concentration with Provost approval). Capstone Project begins — two two-unit courses for exploring and defining the project vision. This is where students start to build deep expertise.

### Year 4 — Synthesis (Final Rotation City + San Francisco)
Students complete Capstone projects and remaining degree requirements. Capstone options include screenplays, technology code, business plans, research papers, or other substantive works. Tutorial courses (up to two per major, except Business) provide collaborative, student-driven exploration. Business majors complete a required summer practicum between years 3 and 4.

**Manifest:** Following spring semester Capstone completion, students present their projects during Manifest — a month-long term in San Francisco celebrating the conclusion of their undergraduate experience. This is a culminating showcase of four years of work.

## Graduate Programs

### Master of Science in Decision Making and Applied Analytics (MDA)
- Transformative, part-time online degree for working professionals
- 21 months duration
- Develops decision-making, analytics, AI, and leadership capabilities
- Serves professionals seeking to advance, change careers, or launch ventures

**Curriculum Pillars:**
1. Systems Thinking and Management Science

**Format:**
- One course unit every other weekend

- 2-2.5 hour live seminars Friday through Sunday with optional flex sessions

**Master's Thesis:** Original 35-page thesis addressing real-world challenges

**AI Integration:** Teaches students to use AI thoughtfully, responsibly, and strategically

**Student Community:**

### Graduate Admissions Process

**Who thrives in this program:**
- Emerging leaders ready for greater responsibility
- Career changers pivoting industries
- Entrepreneurs/innovators scaling ventures
- Global impact seekers (sustainability, equity, policy)
- Lifelong learners committed to critical thinking

**Student Profile:** Age range 23-60; 86% maintain full-time employment while studying

**Application Process (3 stages):**
1. **Application Stage:** Submit academic history, transcripts, CV/resume, and employment status
2. **Exercises:** Complete one 20-minute exercise demonstrating how you think
3. **Interview:** Selected candidates receive online interview invitations

**Deadlines:**
- Round 1: February 10 → Decision March 16 → Enroll by April 15
- Round 2: April 1 → Decision April 20 → Enroll by May 15

**Enrollment deposit:** $1,000 USD (credited toward first tuition payment)

### Graduate Tuition (Fall 2026 Cohort)

**MDA (Master of Science):**
- 36 credits, 5 semesters
- $9,150/semester
- Total: $45,750 (locked at enrollment)

**CDA (Certificate):**
- 24 credits, 3 semesters
- $9,150/semester
- Total: $27,450 (locked at enrollment)

**What tuition covers:** Proprietary virtual learning environment, small interactive classes, Coaching and Talent Development team, professional networking support. Course materials mostly open source — under $100 for entire program.

**Optional Immersion Weeks:** Two one-week programs in cities like London and Seoul. Estimated $2,500 per immersion ($5,000 total). Covers housing, programming, admin. Students pay airfare, local transport, meals.

**Student Loans:** Available to all graduate students not already on scholarship. Covers up to 50% of tuition. Interest rate: 6.5%, APR: 8.14%, Term: 60 months. Contact: financialaid@minerva.edu

**Scholarships:**
- Teach For All: 25% tuition scholarship for network fellows/alumni
- Military Service: 25% tuition scholarship for current/former military with 2+ years active duty or 5+ years reserve duty
